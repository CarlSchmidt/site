About

Hi there. I'm a Paris-based researcher concentrating on planetary atmosphere's at LATMOS. This site contains information about my various projects.

Mercury

Mercury's atmospheric content is comparable to the amount of air in a small building, spread over the whole planet. I use small ground-based telescopes and numerical models to study the sodium component of this atmsophere.

Comets

Cometary atmosphere's are rich in complexity. I've worked on the composition, structure, and dynamics of Comet C/2012 S1 (ISON) using an integral field spectrograph and Comet C/2011 L4 (Pan-STARRS) using the STEREO spacecrafts

Io

Jupiter's volcanic moon Io is a very interesting object. I use the infrared methods to study volcanoes on it's surface. Small telescopes to observe the sodium atmosphereic escape. And the Apache Point Observatory to study a wobbly doughnut of orbiting Jupiter called the Io plasms torus

Europa

Io can potenitally transfere some of it's massively extended atmosphere to the an adjacent moon, Europa.

Moon

Earth's Moon also has a sodium atmosphere similar to Mercury
